I am interested in how populations of cells and organisms — from microbes to cancer — evolve over time. To study the dynamics of evolution, I develop theoretical models, analyze large sets of genomic data, and occasionally do experiments. I am currently involved in several projects, some of which are highlighted below.



Statistics of linked loci

Together with Zhiru Liu and Benjamin Good, I study the properties of two-locus sampling distributions under mutation, selection, recombination, and drift. We use our theoretical predictions to gain insight into the patterns of recombination in bacterial populations.


Population genomics of the spoon-billed sandpiper

I used simulations and mathematical modeling to learn how past changes in the population size of a critically endangered bird species contributed to its present-day genetic diversity. In collaboration with Raimundo Saona, Mateusz Konczal, and Fyodor Kondrashov.
